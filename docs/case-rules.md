# The Rules

> These constraints are non-negotiable.

## 1. No Investment Advice
The experience can inform, explain, contextualize, and help you navigate. It cannot recommend specific investments or tell you what to buy or sell. The investor always decides. This is a legal boundary in self-directed investing, and also a real design challenge: how do you help without being directive?

## 2. No Gamification
No points. No badges. No streaks. No leaderboards. No challenges. Engagement must come from genuine value: making people feel smarter, calmer, more confident, more in control. Financial regulation prohibits gamification in investing apps, and for good reason.

## 3. The Investor Stays in Control
The experience serves the investor. It never manipulates, nudges toward specific actions, or creates urgency. After using your experience, the investor should feel more in control of their decisions.

## 4. Beyond the Chat Bubble
A wall of text is not an adaptive interface. If your solution is just an LLM generating paragraphs, you'll lose. We want the UI itself to change shape. A chatbot waits for you to ask. An adaptive interface figures out what you need and shows it before you ask.
