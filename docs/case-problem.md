# The Problem

Open any investing app today. Bolero, Trade Republic, eToro, your bank's app. They all look the same. A home screen. A search bar. A stock detail page with charts and numbers. An order form. Fixed screens, fixed navigation, one experience for everyone.

The same interface greets:
- A 22-year-old opening the app for the first time with €300 and no idea what a P/E ratio is
- A 55-year-old with €150,000 who just read a headline about a market crash and feels a knot in their stomach
- A confident trader who knows exactly what she wants and just needs speed
- Someone who hasn't opened the app in four months and has no idea what happened to their money

Same home screen. Same stock detail page. Same order form. Everyone pushed through the same corridor.

Investing isn't one experience. It's dozens of different cognitive states: curious, anxious, decisive, confused, overwhelmed, confident, bored. Each one calls for a different interface. Maybe a different kind of interface. Maybe no interface at all.

> Today's apps are databases with buttons. They ask every investor to adapt to the software. What if the software adapted to the investor?
